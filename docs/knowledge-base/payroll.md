# Payroll — Folha de Pagamento

## Salary Rules

### Minimum Wage (Salário Mínimo)
- **2024 national**: R$ 1.412,00 (R$ 6,42/hour)
- Reajuste anual obrigatório — employer MUST update salary when minimum wage changes
- States with regional minimum (higher): SP, RJ, SC, PR, RS

### Salary Registration
- Always register GROSS salary in eSocial
- Annual raise is mandatory when minimum wage increases
- Cannot reduce salary (irredutibilidade salarial — constitutional right)

## Payroll Deductions (Descontos)

### Mandatory Deductions
1. **INSS (Previdência Social)** — Progressive rates:
   - 7.5% — up to R$ 1.412,00
   - 9% — R$ 1.412,01 to R$ 2.666,68
   - 12% — R$ 2.666,69 to R$ 4.000,03
   - 14% — R$ 4.000,04 to R$ 7.786,02
2. **IRRF (Imposto de Renda)** — Variable rates based on salary minus INSS
3. **Vale-transporte** — Up to 6% of base salary (if employee uses public transit)

### Optional/Situational Deductions
- Advances (with signed receipts)
- Health/dental plans (with written agreement)
- Unjustified absences (faltas injustificadas)
- Disciplinary suspensions
- Unpaid aviso prévio
- Consignado loans (with contract documentation)
- Court-ordered alimony (pensão alimentícia)

### What CANNOT Be Deducted
- Food provided at workplace
- Housing (if living on employer's property as condition of employment)
- Hygiene products/uniform costs

## INSS Payment

- Paid via DAE monthly, due by 7th of following month
- Employee contribution: deducted from salary (7.5%-14%)
- Employer contribution: 8% (patronal) — paid by employer, NOT deducted
- Guarantees: retirement, auxílio-doença, salário-maternidade, salário-família

## FGTS

- **8% of gross salary** — paid entirely by employer (not deducted from salary)
- **3.2% FGTS Compensatório** — monthly provision for potential 40% fine on termination
- Deposited in employee's FGTS account at Caixa Econômica Federal
- Since 2015 (LC 150), FGTS became mandatory for domestic workers
- FGTS Digital: new system for managing FGTS deposits

## Employer Total Cost

Beyond salary, employer pays approximately 20% extra:
- 8% FGTS
- 8% INSS patronal
- 3.2% FGTS Compensatório
- 0.8% Seguro acidente de trabalho
- = ~20% on top of gross salary

## Payment Timeline
- Salary: by 5th business day of following month
- DAE: by 7th of following month
- Férias: 2 days before vacation starts
- 13° salário: 1st installment by Nov 30, 2nd by Dec 20

### 2026 Minimum Wage Update
- **National minimum wage 2026**: R$ 1.621,00 (effective January 1, 2026)
- Previous (2025): R$ 1.518,00 — increase of R$ 103,00 (6.78%)
- Policy: annual correction by INPC + real gain based on GDP (0.6%-2.5%)
- First payroll with new value: February 2026 (for January competence)
- FGTS on new minimum: R$ 129.68/month (8% × R$ 1.621,00)

### Regional Minimum Wages
- States with their own (higher) minimums: SP, RJ, SC, PR, RS
- Always check the **state where services are performed**, not where the employer lives
- Regional minimums are usually published later than the national one — watch for retroactive adjustments

### Retroactive Salary Adjustments (Reajuste Retroativo)
- When minimum wage is published after payroll for January is already closed, a retroactive adjustment is required
- Must be registered in eSocial via events S-3500 through S-3507
- Retroactive differences cover: salary, overtime reflexes, vacation already taken, indemnity items, salário-maternidade, and 13° salary
- If employee earns above the new minimum, the raise is negotiated between parties
- Employer must reopen the affected payroll month(s), recalculate, and generate new DAE

### Payment Receipts (Recibos)
- Receipts must be issued for **every payment**: salary, vacation, 13°, termination, advances, vale-transporte
- Must be printed in **two copies** (one for each party), signed by the employee
- Employer must retain signed receipts for **5 years** from payment date
- DAE receipts + bank payment proof: retained for **5 years** from January of the following year
- Incorrect calculations in receipts can lead to labor lawsuits — accuracy is critical

### Payment Date Rules (Detailed)
- **Salary**: 5th business day of the following month (Saturday counts as business day)
- **DAE**: by the 7th (brought forward if weekend/holiday)
- **Vacation**: 2 business days before vacation starts
- **13° 1st installment**: Feb 1 to Nov 30 (or with vacation if requested in January)
- **13° 2nd installment**: by December 20
- **Termination with worked notice**: 1st business day after end of notice period
- **Termination with indemnified notice**: 10 calendar days from notification
- **Justa causa**: 10 calendar days from termination date

### True Cost of a Domestic Worker (2026 estimate)
- Base salary: R$ 1.621,00
- Employer taxes (~20%): R$ 324,20
- Monthly provisioning for vacation + 1/3: ~R$ 180,11
- Monthly provisioning for 13°: ~R$ 135,08
- Vale-transporte (employer portion): varies
- **Total estimated monthly cost**: ~R$ 2,260+ (minimum wage scenario)
